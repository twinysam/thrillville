Thrilltastic!
by Sam

More than a year passed since my last ramble in the subject. I still enjoy the slight share of fun I find in using Thrillville as a way to describe my thoughts on LucasArts.
Now the question could be -is LucasArts off the rails?- pretty obvious one eh?
If being a money making machine is being on the rails, then yes, LucasArts seems to be doing quite fine this days. There is a whole new Star Wars thingy called The Clone Wars (which we already saw actually, but what the hell, GO CGI!), and that means new games. There is also the much anticipated Wii lightsaber-esque The Force Unleashed, and the upcoming brand-new Star Wars MMO The Old Republic.
I think the latest LucasArts original (as in "new IP", not as in "creative") game since Thrillville is Fracture (a very masculine third-person shooter).
The truth is that LucasArts isn't even making most of this games. They are all made by other studios to be distributed by LucasArts, there is very little to no in-house development.

Bottom line, I was fooling myself if actually had any hope on LucasArts. To be honest I never really had any hope, I just wanted new generations of LucasArts-loving kids to know the truth. In the process I got cought in a hopeless torrent of false hope, which hopefully is already gone by now.
You won't find hope on LucasArts here and I will not tell you to ask Darrell Rodriguez (the crashtastic new LucasArts president) to do more creative and original games (because he probably won't, basically).

What I plan to do this time is very simple and unambitious. I'll tell you which are the two games I'm really looking forward to, which happen to be rather unknown. If I manage to get you excited about them it'll be thrilltastic!

MACHINARIUM
Created by Amanita Design
Official Site

This game looks very very promising to me. A point-&-click adventure about an unwanted little robot. Hand-drawn art, hand-drawn animation, exquisit art direction. There is no dialog, and the story will be completely game-driven (and this guys are great at it).
Since I played Amanita Design's two previous games Samorost and Samorost 2, I fell in love with them. You can play both of them online and for free, although Samorost 2 you can only play the first part, you'll have to buy it to play the whole thing (which I recommend).
Not much more has been revealed of Machinarium, except it will be probably coming out (early, hopefully) next year.

LIMBO
Created by Arnt Jensen
Official site

There is little to no information about this game. It seems to be a cinematic platformer (with noir aesthetics) which reminds me of Eric Chahi's last two games (which I love).
Arnt Jensen is the game designer and artistic genius behind what looks like a very promising game. For what I read, Limbo received some considerable funding from the Danish government.
The game has been in development since 2006, and as of late 2008 there is no news of it's progress. But i'm sure there's been progress as this is the kind of project which is a labor of love and (unlike Machinarium, but like the Samorost games) done in the creators' spare time.
I hope that during 2009, news about the game come to light.
Watch this teaser and drop your jaw:

So there you go. Two awesome-looking games that I'm really looking forward to. It's also curious to note that none of the two developers are North Americans, and they come from countries which have little history of video game wonders (that I know of). Times are changing, and for good apparently. Now days it seems it's easier for independent developers to distribute their own games with services such as Steam and GameTap, and that is a good thing (for PC games at least).

I hope you liked this two games (if you didn't you might as well go and stick your head in a bucket full of vomit), and I see you when there is news about any of them, or when LucasArts releases a new Thrillville game.

You can write and tell me what you think.
Farewell O human child.
